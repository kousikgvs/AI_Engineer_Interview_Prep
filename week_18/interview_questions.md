# Week 18 — Interview Questions: Capstone, System Design & Hiring Sprint

> Sourced from onsite loops. This week is where system-design and behavioral rounds concentrate.

---

## AI-Native System Design (whiteboard drills)
1. **Design a system like Perplexity** (search + retrieval + citations + streaming) end-to-end.
2. **Design a production RAG system** with proper retrieval, reranking, and evaluation.
3. **Design a multi-agent coding assistant** and explain how you prevent cascading failures.
4. **Design an LLM gateway** with routing, caching, cost optimization, and fallbacks.
5. Design an AI system handling 10M daily requests — walk through the architecture.
6. What is an Architecture Decision Record (ADR) and what goes in one?
7. What is unique about AI system design vs traditional software system design?

## Product Engineering
8. Customer discovery — what is Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)?
9. Product metrics: activation, retention, engagement, NPS — which matter for an AI product?
10. Unit economics: cost per query and margin — how do you model them?
11. Feature prioritization: ICE vs RICE.
12. How do you A/B test an AI feature with significance?

## AI Product Intuition
13. Why did Cursor / Perplexity / Claude Code win? What would you do differently?

## Distribution & Hiring
14. How does OSS contribution act as a hiring signal?
15. How do you write a technical blog post that demonstrates depth?
16. What makes a technical demo sell?

## Capstone Deliverables (be ready to present)
17. Walk me through your capstone system design.
18. How did you evaluate your system? Show your eval harness.
19. What failed during development and what would you do differently?
20. Show me a production deployment (not localhost).

## Judgment
21. "Build vs buy — host your own model or use the API?" — defend it.
